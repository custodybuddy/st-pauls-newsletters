Greetings Friends

 

Welcome to the April 2026 edition of the Banner.

As the spring sun begins to warm our community here in Ingersoll, we find ourselves deeply immersed in the joy and promise of the Easter season. Following our powerful "Resurrection and the Life" service on April 5th, we are reminded of the strategic importance of gathering as a church family.

Our core theme this month is one of profound transformation: moving from the "grave clothes" of our past into the new life promised by Christ. Just as Lazarus was called forth from the tomb, we are being invited to shed the heavy wrappings of our old selves and walk in the freedom of God's grace.

Our Mission

We are a Christ-centered, worshipping community serving and leading people to Jesus.

St. Paul's Ministry Spotlight

Relationships Ministry

The Connective Tissue of Our Community

 

Faith is a journey best shared. The Relationships Ministry focuses on building the "connective tissue" within our church and our town.

We are proud to be the "home-base" for the Ingersoll & District Inter-Church efforts, providing assistance with food, rent, and utilities to our neighbours in need.

Whether through our Session providing spiritual leadership or the Board of Managers finding ways to fund our congregational goals, we work together to ensure no one walks alone.

The Resurrection and the Life

 

The Easter narrative, as chronicled in Luke 24 and John 11, serves as the cornerstone for our hope here in Ingersoll. In our study of Luke, we were reminded that the first witnesses to the empty tomb — Mary Magdalene, Joanna, and Mary the mother of James — remained courageous and servant-hearted even when others were in hiding.

During the Easter message, Rev. Allan Brouwer offered a powerful first-person dramatic monologue, speaking from the perspective of Lazarus. He described the "anguish of the grave" and the specific moment his name was called — reminding us that we are all known by name by the One who holds authority over death.

Easter Sunday · April 5, 2026 "Take off your grave clothes — and live for God's glory." — Rev. Allan Brouwer, from the perspective of Lazarus

We also celebrate a moment of beautiful resilience from our Easter service: when a technical challenge erupted from our soundboard early Sunday morning, Peter and the tech crew pivoted instantly — even hauling equipment from downstairs to protect the live stream. No technical hurdle can diminish the celebration of a Risen Lord.

Compassionate Response: Serving Our Neighbours

 

Outreach programs like Linens for Lent are vital expressions of our identity at St. Paul's. Our recent Spaghetti Supper was a testament to this — our volunteers used their whole selves as instruments of righteousness, as described in Romans 6:13. By serving our neighbours, we give ourselves completely to God's work.

🧺 Linens for Lent — A Success!

Following our Palm Sunday service, our congregation delivered a substantial collection to Mission Services of London. Your generosity provided:

• Gently-used towels, sheets, face cloths & dish cloths
• Kitchen essentials: pots, pans, dishes, cutlery & glasses
• Immediate support for families leaving domestic abuse situations to start over

As we continue to act as Christ's hands and feet, we find that our service must be grounded in a deep and consistent study of the Word of God.

Finding Home in the Courts of the Lord

 

During our recent services, we shared a responsive reading of Psalm 84. We reflected on the imagery of the sparrow finding a home and the swallow a nest "where she may have her young" near the altar of the Lord. For many in our congregation, St. Paul's serves as that sanctuary — a place of belonging for families and individuals alike.

"The Lord God is like the sun that gives us light;
he is like a shield that keeps us safe."
— Psalm 84:11

We hold fast to the promise that God doesn't hold back anything good from those whose lives are without blame. This assurance guides our path as we seek to be a blameless witness in our community.
