# DESIGN.md - Newsletter Analysis: St. Paul's April 2026

## 1. Aesthetic Identity
- **Theme**: "Resurrection & Transformation" (The transition from 'Grave Clothes' to 'New Life').
- **Visual Style**: Clean, Card-based UI with Liturgical accents.
- **Palette**: 
    - **Navy (#1a2a40)**: Mission statements and headers.
    - **Gold (#d4af37)**: Key emphasis and branding.
    - **Cream/Off-White**: Background for soft visual texture.

## 2. Information Hierarchy
- **Primary Focus**: The "Mission" card acts as the visual anchor.
- **Middle Layer**: Ministry Spotlights (Relationships, Compassionate Response).
- **Practical Layer**: Financial Stewardship and Upcoming Events (Logistics).
- **Gratitude Layer**: Volunteer spotlights to foster community belonging.

## 3. Notable Components
- **The Ministry Hub Diagram**: A circular visualization of the 5 pillars (Worship, Stewardship, Relationships, Equipping, Compassionate Care).
- **Linens for Lent Success Box**: Quantitative reporting (Gently-used towels, kitchen essentials) to show tangible impact.
- **Financial Shortfall Highlight**: Transparent reporting to justify the call to action for donations.

## 4. Typography & UX
- **Serif Headers**: Conveys tradition and clerical authority.
- **Sans-Serif Body**: Ensures high readability for a diverse congregation.
- **Call-to-Action**: The "Give via E-Transfer" button is high-contrast goldenrod for maximum conversion.
- **Contact Info**: Clearly defined footer with physical address, office hours, and digital links (YouTube/Website).
