Pastoral Note

☀️ Greetings Friends

Welcome to spring! It's been a long winter, but we made it through, and we are now in that wonderful time of year where we can look forward to the sunshine, the longer days, and just being outside.

As we continue in our newsletter series, we would like to focus on another new ministry at St. Paul's — our "Equipping Ministry."

This team is really just getting started, and our focus will be to look at how we can continue to strengthen the relationships we have at St. Paul's, as we grow in faith together, and how we welcome new members, etc.

Our Mission

We are a Christ-centered, worshipping community serving and leading people to Jesus.

St. Paul's Ministry Spotlight

🤝 Equipping Ministry

Also, a big focus this year will be to discern how we can reach out and bless our community. We know that society has changed, and for many, a relationship with a church, or even knowing Jesus, is not part of their lives. As a result, people aren't just walking through our doors.

Yet, we know it's a hurting world, and so many people are struggling to find hope, especially when the challenges come. People are searching for purpose and meaning, and a sense of belonging.

We would also like to start reaching out to the community to determine if there are unmet needs that we could tackle, or if other groups could benefit from a partner. It's asking where we can be the hands and feet of Christ in our community?

Community Needs

💡 Did You Know?

Three local realities are shaping how St. Paul’s can serve with purpose in the community.

01. Food Support

The Salvation Army Food Bank has seen the number of people seeking food support double in recent years. Perhaps we could strengthen our support of this very important place in our community? Food insecurity has become such a problem across this country.

02. School-Year Care

The "Lunch Bunch" prepares a weekly lunch for approximately 270 children in our community during the school year. Have you ever thought about what happens to these children during the summer? There is a new venture in Ingersoll through The Food Project that is tackling this issue. Perhaps we could add strength there?

03. Family Support

So many families are struggling today. Perhaps there are ways that we can walk beside them and let them know they are not alone on their journey.

Strategic Discernment

🌍 Our Mission in the Community

These are questions we will talk about and pray about as we begin our work as a new ministry.

St. Paul's has a presence on the main street of Ingersoll, and we'd love to make Ingersoll notice this presence in a greater way. We want St. Paul's to matter to Ingersoll! But this is not about us.

It's about discerning how we can reach out and care for others and shine the light of Jesus for them; perhaps making them want to know more about St. Paul's and our mission.

Biblical Foundations

✨ Experiencing God
Study Reflection

"When a church sees where God is at work, this is its invitation to join Him."

— Experiencing God Study

In the 'Experiencing God' bible study, we were reminded that God is at work ALL the time, and He invites us to become involved in His work.

We also learned that God has brought each one of us to St. Paul's for a reason, and He uses each one of us to do His work. He may be speaking to you or giving you insights into His vision for St. Paul's.

Maybe you've not wanted to share this, or known what to do with it, but we'd love to hear about it. So many amazing ideas and projects started with the dream of one person inspired by God.

We want to pray for God's discernment where He is working and how He wants us to be part of His assignment. We know we will never be able to accomplish what God wants us to do on our own, but by working together, using our God-given gifts and talents, with God's help, we can do amazing things!

Stewardship Update

📊 Focus on Finances

At our annual meeting in February, we committed to supporting an annual budget of $208,127 in 2026. We must operate with a balanced budget, so this means we can expect monthly expenses of $17,343.91, supported and balanced by equal revenues.

As of March 31, 2026 (3 months in), our financial status is as follows:

Total Income

$44,432.80

Total Expenses

$56,726.60

Shortfall

-$12,293.70

To provide some insight on the above, we have had some higher than budgeted expenses in our Ground Maintenance and Natural Gas year to date. Given our cold, snowy winter, we understand these expenses.

While we did see increases in our givings in February, we did fall behind in March. This has led to the current shortfall.

At our annual meeting, we shared the following chart which outlined scenarios that could help us eliminate* the recurring pattern of an operating deficit.

Youth Opportunity

🌱 Youth Support Funds Available

The Presbytery of Paris has funds available to support youth in their spiritual development, as well as academic pursuits.

The Presbytery may provide funding for youth involved in its congregations to attend camps, workshops, or Christian events that will support their faith journey.

Funds are also available for bursaries or grants to help finance further education for post-secondary students, to assist them as they pursue study at colleges or universities.

Questions?

To find out more about the criteria to apply for these funds, you can contact the church office.
